# product-sales-analysis

# Project Oviewer
- This project analyzes product-level sales data to identify top-performing and underperforming products, uncover sales patterns, and provide actionable business recommendations.
- The goal is to support data-driven decision-making by understanding how different products contribute to overall sales performance.

# Objectives
- Identify top-selling products.
- Identify low-performing products.
- Analyze sales distribution across products.
- Provide business recommendations to improve performance.

# Tools Used
- Microsoft Excel.
- Pivot Tables.
- Data Sorting & Filtering.
- Charts (Bar Graphs).

# Data Analysis Process
- Created pivot tables to summarize sales by product.
- Sorted products from highest to lowest sales.
- Applied Top 10 filter to identify best-performing products.
- Applied Bottom 10 filter to identify low-performing products.
- Visualized results using bar charts for better interpretation.

# Key Insight
- Sales are distributed across multiple top-performing products, with no single product dominating overall performance.
- Top products show relatively similar sales values, indicating shared contribution to total revenue.
- A significant gap exists between top-performing and low-performing products, highlighting uneven performance across the product range.
- A large number of products contribute minimally to total sales, reflecting a Pareto-like distribution pattern

# Recommendation
- Maintain strong support (marketing, stock availability) for top-performing products to maximize revenue.
- Investigate low-performing products to identify causes of weak sales and potential improvement strategies.
- Leverage characteristics of top-performing products to improve performance of weaker products.
- Consider reviewing or discontinuing consistently underperforming products.
